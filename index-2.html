THAILAND

Bangkok, Thailand’s capital, is a sprawling metropolis known for its ornate shrines and vibrant street life.

The boat-filled Chao Phraya River feeds its network of canals, flowing past the Rattanakosin royal district, home to the opulent Grand Palace and its sacred Emerald Buddha temple.

Istanbul

TURKEY

Istanbul is a city in Turkey that straddles Europe and Asia across the Bosphorus Strait.

The Old City reflects cultural influences of the many empires that once ruled here. In the Sultanahmet district, the open-air, Roman-era Hippodrome was for centuries the site of chariot races, and Egyptian obelisks remain. The iconic Byzantine Hagia Sophia features a soaring dome and Christian mosaics.

Cape town

SOUTH AFRICA

Cape Town is a port city on South Africa’s southwest coast, on a peninsula beneath the imposing Table Mountain.

Slowly rotating cable cars climb to the mountain’s flat top, from which there are sweeping views of the city, the busy harbor and boats heading for Robben Island in Table Bay. The notorious prison that once held Nelson Mandela is now a living museum.

Paris

FRANCE

Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy and culture.

Its picturesque 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is known for its cafe culture, and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

London

UNITED KINGDOM

London, England’s capital, set on the River Thames, is a 21st-century city with history stretching back to Roman times.

At its centre stand the imposing Houses of Parliament, the iconic ‘Big Ben’ clock tower and Westminster Abbey, site of British monarch coronations. Across the Thames, the London Eye observation wheel provides panoramic views of the South Bank cultural complex, and the entire city.
